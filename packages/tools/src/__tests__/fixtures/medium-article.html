How we scale our frontend architecture

by Jane Doe · 12 min read

Over the last eighteen months our engineering team has wrestled with an increasingly thorny problem: how do we let hundreds of product engineers ship features in parallel without the frontend codebase collapsing under the weight of its own success?

The short answer is boring: clear module boundaries, a small and opinionated core team, and relentless investment in tooling. The long answer takes the rest of this post.

Principle 1 · Boundaries before frameworks

Every time we tried to solve scale with a shiny new framework we ended up replacing one set of pain points with another. What actually worked was treating our system as a graph of modules with explicit contracts, enforced by build-time linting.

Principle 2 · Tooling is a product

We pulled a senior IC off product work for six months to own developer experience. The incremental build time dropped by 74%, and so did our incident count.

Reliable, fast tooling is not a luxury. It is the single largest leverage point an engineering org has once the team crosses ~30 people.

Conclusion

Scaling frontend engineering is a social problem dressed up as a technical one. Solve the social part first and the tech will follow.
